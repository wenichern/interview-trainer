## Undergraduate Research & Internship Support

### University of Warwick — URSS (Undergraduate Research Support Scheme)

Warwick has a very well-established formal program:

- **6–10 week** self-directed, non-assessed research project (usually over summer, July–September)
- Supervised by a Warwick academic
- **Bursary of up to £1,500** to cover living expenses
- Open to **all undergraduates in all faculties**, including first-years
- Can be done on campus, at home, or **abroad**
- Projects can be in a different department from your degree
- Strong outcomes: **70% of URSS participants earned a first-class degree** vs. 35% of students overall
- Annual showcase event for students to present their work
- Next application cycle: opens October 2026, deadline February 2027
- Contact: urss@warwick.ac.uk

### University of Edinburgh

- Edinburgh is ranked **29th globally** (vs. Warwick at 122nd) and has a **Research Quality score of 94.8** (vs. Warwick's 85.6)
- Receives **over £200 million annually** in research income — one of the highest in the UK
- 20 affiliated Nobel laureates
- Ranked **=1st globally for Industry, Innovation and Infrastructure** (THE Impact Rankings)
- Edinburgh offers research opportunities through individual schools/departments (e.g., vacation research scholarships in Biology, Chemistry, Physics, etc.)
- The university encourages study/work abroad experiences and has a **Go Abroad Fund** providing grants for 250+ students per year
- Being a major Russell Group research university, many departments embed research into the undergraduate curriculum, especially in the 4th year of Scottish honours degrees (dissertation/research project)

## Summary

- **Edinburgh** is the stronger research university overall (ranked 29th globally), with a better student-to-staff ratio (11.3 vs 13.2) and significantly higher research quality scores. It offers research opportunities through individual departments, and its 4-year Scottish degree structure includes substantial research in the final year.

- **Warwick** has a more structured, centralized undergraduate research program (URSS) with funded summer placements and a bursary. It also holds Gold TEF rating across all teaching categories, and Warwick's Business & Economics ranks higher than Edinburgh's (35th vs 60th). Warwick is also more highly targeted by UK graduate employers.

Both are excellent choices — Edinburgh edges ahead on global prestige, research intensity, and staff ratio, while Warwick offers a more formalized and well-funded undergraduate research support scheme plus strong graduate employability.
