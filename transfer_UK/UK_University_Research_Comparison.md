## Undergraduate Research & Internship Support

### University of Warwick — URSS (Undergraduate Research Support Scheme)

Warwick has a very well-established formal program:

- **6–10 week** self-directed, non-assessed research project (usually over summer, July–September)
- Supervised by a Warwick academic
- **Bursary of up to £1,500** to cover living expenses
- Open to **all undergraduates in all faculties**, including first-years
- Can be done on campus, at home, or **abroad**
- Projects can be in a different department from your degree
- Strong outcomes: **70% of URSS participants earned a first-class degree** vs. 35% of students overall
- Annual showcase event for students to present their work
- Next application cycle: opens October 2026, deadline February 2027
- Contact: urss@warwick.ac.uk

### University of Edinburgh

- Edinburgh is ranked **29th globally** (vs. Warwick at 122nd) and has a **Research Quality score of 94.8** (vs. Warwick's 85.6)
- Receives **over £200 million annually** in research income — one of the highest in the UK
- 20 affiliated Nobel laureates
- Ranked **=1st globally for Industry, Innovation and Infrastructure** (THE Impact Rankings)
- Edinburgh offers research opportunities through individual schools/departments (e.g., vacation research scholarships in Biology, Chemistry, Physics, etc.)
- The university encourages study/work abroad experiences and has a **Go Abroad Fund** providing grants for 250+ students per year
- Being a major Russell Group research university, many departments embed research into the undergraduate curriculum, especially in the 4th year of Scottish honours degrees (dissertation/research project)

### UC Berkeley — College of Letters & Science (L&S)

Berkeley is the **#1 public research university in the world** with massive biomedical research infrastructure — **but most of these resources are designed for and prioritized for graduate students and PhDs, not undergraduates.**

- **THE World Ranking: 9th** (vs. Edinburgh 29th, Warwick 122nd) — Research Quality score of **98.9**
- Annual research expenditure: **over $1 billion** — one of the highest in the world
- **25+ Nobel laureates** on faculty (19 in physics, chemistry, economics)

#### ⚠️ Reality Check: Undergraduate Research Access at Berkeley

> **Based on Berkeley student feedback**: The vast majority of Berkeley's research infrastructure — its world-class labs, institutes, and centers — is **primarily for graduate students, postdocs, and PhD candidates**. Undergraduates face significant barriers to meaningful research participation.

**URAP (Undergraduate Research Apprentice Program)**:
- On paper: 1,500+ students participate per semester
- **Reality**: URAP is **highly competitive** — PIs and grad students receive dozens to hundreds of applications per project and filter aggressively by GPA and connections
- Students with prior REU experience and published papers still get rejected
- Sub-3.0 GPA students are often automatically filtered out
- **Unpaid** — students earn only course credit (1 unit per 3 hours), no stipend
- Undergrad roles are often limited to **basic tasks**: data entry, cleaning equipment, running simple protocols — not genuine independent research
- Many students describe URAP as a "resume line" rather than a deep research experience
- Cold-emailing professors is the alternative, but faculty are overwhelmed and rarely respond to undergrads

**Additional Undergraduate Research Programs** (limited spots):
- **SURF (Summer Undergraduate Research Fellowship)** — funded summer research, but very competitive
- **Haas Scholars Program** — only ~20 awards per year ($6,000 each), seniors only
- **McNair Scholars Program** — limited to first-generation/low-income students
- **MARC Program** — limited to underrepresented minority students in biomedical sciences

**Key Biomedical/Medicine Research Centers** (⚠️ mostly graduate/PhD-focused):
- **QB3 (California Institute for Quantitative Biosciences)** — primarily for grad students and postdocs; limited undergrad access
- **Li Ka Shing Center for Biomedical and Health Sciences** — graduate research facility
- **Berkeley-UCSF Joint Medical Program (JMP)** — this is a **graduate** MD program, not available to undergrads
- **Innovative Genomics Institute (IGI)** — CRISPR research led by grad students and postdocs; rare undergrad involvement
- **Helen Wills Neuroscience Institute** — graduate-focused
- **Henry Wheeler Center for Emerging and Neglected Diseases** — graduate research

**What IS available to undergrads:**
- **MCB 99/199 Independent Research** — course credit for lab work, but requires finding a faculty sponsor (competitive)
- **MCB Honors Program** — thesis-based, senior year only
- **Harris Research Discovery Program** — for MCB undergrads
- Large introductory lectures (300–800 students) with graduate student-led discussion sections
- Pre-med advising through Health Professions Advising Office
- Robinson Life Science, Business, and Entrepreneurship Program

### Cost Notes
- **UC Berkeley**: Out-of-state tuition includes base tuition (~$15,500) + Nonresident Supplemental Tuition ($39,270). Living costs in Berkeley/Bay Area are extremely high (~$40,000/yr for housing, food, personal, and transport). California residents pay ~$15,500/yr tuition, reducing the 4-year total to ~$220,000.
- **Edinburgh**: Tuition for international students in science programs. Scottish 4-year honours degrees include a full research dissertation in Year 4.
- **Warwick**: 3-year English degree is one year shorter, saving an entire year of living costs + tuition. Tuition for international students in science/biomedical programs.
- All UK institutions charge an **NHS Immigration Health Surcharge** (£776/yr) which gives full NHS access.
- UC Berkeley charges for **Student Health Insurance Plan (SHIP)** — can be waived with equivalent coverage.

## Summary

### For Biomedical / Medicine Undergraduate Resources:

> ⚠️ **Critical distinction**: University rankings reflect overall research output (mostly driven by graduate/PhD programs). For an **undergraduate** student, what matters is what you can actually access and participate in.

**🥇 University of Edinburgh** — Best overall for biomedical undergraduates:
- Ranked **29th globally**, **=20th in Medical & Health**, **32nd in Life Sciences**
- **Best student-to-staff ratio** of the three (11.3:1) — most personal attention
- **Edinburgh Medical School and Royal Infirmary on campus** — real clinical exposure possible for undergrads
- **4-year Scottish degree includes a mandatory research dissertation year** — every student does genuine research
- Vacation research scholarships available through departments
- Smaller class sizes and personal tutor system
- Mid-range cost at ~$246,000 for 4 years
- **Drawback**: Research opportunities less formalized than Warwick's URSS; requires initiative to find vacation placements before Year 4

**🥈 University of Warwick** — Best value + best structured undergraduate research:
- Ranked **122nd globally**, **126–150th in Medical & Health**
- **URSS is the standout**: structured, funded (£1,500 bursary), open to ALL undergrads including first-years, faculty-supervised self-directed project
- **70% of URSS participants earned a first-class degree** vs 35% overall
- Warwick Medical School on campus — undergrads can access health/biomedical research
- Gold TEF rating — strong teaching quality
- Most affordable: **~$168,500 for the full 3-year degree**
- **Drawback**: Lower global ranking in Medical & Health and Life Sciences; smaller overall research footprint

**🥉 UC Berkeley (L&S)** — Best on paper, but limited for undergrads in practice:
- Ranked **9th globally**, **10th in Medical & Health**, **8th in Life Sciences** — but these rankings reflect **graduate/PhD research output**
- Over **$1 billion** in research expenditure — but **most goes to grad students, postdocs, and PhD candidates**
- World-class centers (IGI, QB3, Li Ka Shing) exist on campus but are **largely inaccessible to undergrads**
- URAP is unpaid (credit only), highly competitive, and often involves basic tasks rather than real research
- **No medical school on campus** — JMP is a graduate program; UCSF clinical access is for grad students
- Intro bio lectures have **300–800 students**; student-to-staff ratio of 18.4:1
- By far the most expensive: **~$400,000 for 4 years** (out-of-state), with ~$40K/yr living costs
- **Strengths for undergrads**: Excellent pre-med advising office, MCB department offers honors thesis for seniors, Robinson Life Science Program, Berkeley name recognition for med school applications

### Bottom Line

While UC Berkeley's **university-level** research resources dwarf Edinburgh and Warwick, the reality for **undergraduate students** is very different. Most of Berkeley's $1B+ research infrastructure serves graduate and PhD students. Edinburgh and Warwick offer undergraduates **more direct, structured, and accessible** research opportunities — especially Warwick's URSS (funded, open to all years) and Edinburgh's mandatory 4th-year research dissertation.

For a student focused on biomedical/medicine preparation as an undergraduate, **Edinburgh offers the best balance** of research access, clinical exposure (Medical School + Royal Infirmary), personal attention, and cost. **Warwick offers the best value** with the most accessible structured research program.
