## 💡 Key Takeaways

1. **Dalhousie is the best value** at ~$30,100 USD/year — nearly **half** the cost of U of T.
2. **Alberta universities (U of Alberta, U of Calgary)** offer excellent value at ~$35,000 USD/year with strong medical school affiliations.
3. **McGill remains competitive** at ~$37,000 USD/year despite the 2024 Quebec fee increase — Montreal is also one of Canada's most affordable cities for students.
4. **Toronto and Vancouver are expensive** — both have high tuition AND high cost of living (rent is nearly double that of other cities).
5. **The CAD/USD exchange rate is your friend** — all costs are effectively ~28% cheaper when converted from CAD to USD.
6. **Canadian 4-year degrees align perfectly with AMCAS** — no degree equivalency issues unlike UK 3-year degrees.
7. **Winter clothing is essential** — budget $500–$1,000 CAD for a proper winter coat, boots, and layers, especially for Edmonton, Calgary, Ottawa, Kingston, and Halifax.
8. **Study permit financial requirement**: You must demonstrate **$20,635 CAD + first year tuition** in available funds to obtain your Canadian study permit.
9. **Working while studying**: International students can work up to **20 hours/week** during term — this can offset $5,000–$8,000 CAD/year.
10. **Total 4-year degree cost** ranges from **~$120,000 USD (Dalhousie) to ~$244,000 USD (U of T)**.
