## 💡 Key Takeaways

1. **Dalhousie is the best value** at ~$30,900 USD/year — less than **half** the cost of U of T.
2. **Alberta universities (U of Alberta, U of Calgary)** offer excellent value at ~$35,000 USD/year with strong medical school affiliations.
3. **McGill is no longer a bargain** — after Quebec's 2024 international fee overhaul, McGill's science tuition jumped from ~$22K to ~$42K CAD, putting it on par with Western (~$44,200 USD/year total).
4. **Ontario universities have surged** — Queen's ($54K CAD) and McMaster ($51K CAD) tuition are significantly higher than a few years ago, making them comparable to UBC.
5. **Toronto and Vancouver are the most expensive** — U of T at ~$68,900 USD/year and UBC at ~$60,300 USD/year combine high tuition with high cost of living.
6. **The CAD/USD exchange rate is your friend** — all costs are effectively ~28% cheaper when converted from CAD to USD.
7. **Canadian 4-year degrees align perfectly with AMCAS** — no degree equivalency issues unlike UK 3-year degrees.
8. **Winter clothing is essential** — budget $500–$1,000 CAD for a proper winter coat, boots, and layers, especially for Edmonton, Calgary, Ottawa, Kingston, and Halifax.
9. **Study permit financial requirement**: You must demonstrate **$20,635 CAD + first year tuition** in available funds to obtain your Canadian study permit.
10. **Working while studying**: International students can work up to **20 hours/week** during term — this can offset $5,000–$8,000 CAD/year.
11. **Total 4-year degree cost** ranges from **~$124,000 USD (Dalhousie) to ~$276,000 USD (U of T)**.
